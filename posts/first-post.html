Why I Made This

I didn’t build this to make a pretty website. I built it because I needed a place that could hold the weight of how I actually think.

I’m not someone who just writes stories and leaves them on the page. My mind builds systems. It builds rooms. It builds environments that keep hold of things and don’t let them evaporate the second somebody looks away. That’s always been closer to how I work, and closer to how I survive.

I’m a multidisciplinary creator from Bristol, and a lot of what I make comes out of friction. Friction with the world. Friction with systems. Friction with the long list of struggles that come with being me — the auADHD, the dyslexia, the psychotic depression, the anxiety, and everything else that seems to pile in at once. Sane or insane, I’m still just a bit of me.

So this site exists because I was done with building things that felt too clean to be true. I wanted somewhere that could think properly. Somewhere that could carry research, dark narrative, spiritual restlessness, hidden history, technical design, and the private logic that sits underneath all of it.

Sanity & Secrets is my answer to that. It’s an archive, yes, but not a dead one. It’s a system. A chamber. A record of thought under pressure. A place where truth-seeking and secrecy can exist in the same structure without cancelling each other out.

I’ve always been drawn to the hidden layer of things — the part beneath the official explanation, the part that still hums after everyone else has accepted the version they were given. History does that. Institutions do that. People do that. The internet definitely does that. This site is built from that instinct: the need to look under the floorboards and keep going when something down there answers back.

Part of this is personal, and part of it is architectural. I wanted to create a space that felt persistent. Not disposable. Not content-churn. Not a stream of posts that vanish into the algorithmic swamp five minutes after they’re born. I wanted a site that behaves more like an environment than a feed. A place where entering it means something.

That matters to me because survival has always felt systemic. Internal systems. External systems. Belief systems. Government systems. The body’s own broken systems. The structures we live inside and the structures we build to stop ourselves collapsing. I think the best writing comes from noticing that, and from refusing to pretend the surface layer is the whole story.

So this is where I’m putting it all. The writing. The research. The philosophy. The worldbuilding. The contradictions. The dark humour. The things I can prove and the things I can only feel at the edges. It won’t always be tidy. I don’t want it to be. I want it to be alive enough to hold complexity.

If you’ve found this place, then welcome. Properly welcome. Take what helps. Ignore what doesn’t. Follow what feels true. And if any of it feels a little too close to the bone, that probably means I wrote it honestly.

— Lord G.S.T
